About Me

Jose Castro

Hi, my name is Jose, I’m originally from Ecuador, but I currently live in Sandy, Utah with my wife and our 8-month-old son. I’ve been happily married for almost 6 years, and honestly, my wife and son are my biggest motivation—they’re the reason I keep pushing forward, even when life gets busy or tough.

I recently earned my Associate’s degree in Business Management, and I decided to continue my education by pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Web Design and Development (WDD) here at BYU-Idaho. My goal is to transition into a career in web development, combining both the creative and logical sides of my personality to build tools, websites, and digital solutions that can help people.

Some of the things I love doing in my free time include drawing, writing stories, and working on personal projects—especially ones that involve technology and creativity. I also enjoy cooking with the air fryer, keeping up with tech trends, and brainstorming side hustle ideas that might one day turn into something bigger.

Right now, I’m balancing school with work and family life. Pursuing this degree is a step toward a career change—one that gives me the flexibility and potential to create something of my own in the future.

One of the things I love most about BYU-Idaho is the way it combines spiritual values with professional learning. The community here is supportive, and I appreciate how faith is integrated into the learning experience.

This course is especially exciting because web development is a skill I’ve wanted to learn for a long time. I see a lot of opportunities in tech, and I believe learning how to design and build websites will help me create projects for others, build a career, and even support my family in new ways.

I’ve been a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints my whole life. One of my favorite quotes comes from President Russell M. Nelson:

“The joy we feel has little to do with the circumstances of our lives and everything to do with the focus of our lives.”

My favorite scripture is Mosiah 2:17:

"When ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God."

It reminds me that every skill I learn can be used to serve others, whether it’s helping someone build a website or just being a dependable husband and father.

I’m excited to keep learning, grow my skills, and connect with others in this class. I believe this journey will bless my family and give me the tools to make a difference—both spiritually and professionally.
